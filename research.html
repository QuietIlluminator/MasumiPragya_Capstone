  • leading melanoma detection devices have not advanced much recently, especially since dermatologists are slow to adapt changes

  • the procedures for determining melanoma at the moment are very invasive, and there are alternate procedures that have been proven to be effective in diagnosing melanoma

  • thermal conductivity has been proven effective in diagnosing melanoma accurately

  • however, this thermal conductivity approach has not yet been fully combined with previously established approaches

  • the HAM10000 dataset is a reputable Harvard-ran dataset for common pigmented skin lesions

  • melanoma diagnoses are accurate using deep learning techniques on dermatoscopic images

  • thus, by combining the previously established methods of deep learning with melanoma imaging and thermal conductivity, we aim to establish proof of concept regarding more accurate melanoma detection with less invasive methods utilizing a simple scanning device
